Before you begin

This is a 60-question comprehensive master exam spanning all seven Intro to PA Profession lecture decks — History of the PA Profession, PA Organizations, Professional Practice, Health Care is Teamwork, PAs and Clinical Practice, Professionalism, and Difficult Conversations. It is one of five parallel forms (A–E) of the same master exam, each drawing a different 60-question set from the same shared question pool, so no two forms are identical.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Trace the history of the PA profession, from its physician-shortage origins and the Duke corpsmen program through the five stages of evolutionary development to today's licensure-compact era.
  • 2Describe the roles and missions of the profession's national organizations — ARC-PA, PAEA, NCCPA, AAPA, SAAAPA, PAF, and FAPA — and how they interconnect.
  • 3Identify the certification, licensure, credentialing, reimbursement, and legal/regulatory rules governing day-to-day PA practice, including PANCE, Florida licensure, and Optimal Team Practice.
  • 4Recognize the roles of other health care team members and the value of interprofessional collaboration, patient-centered care, and clinical practice settings available to PAs.
  • 5Apply the principles of professionalism, academic and intellectual integrity, and structured communication frameworks (NURSE, SPIKES, CUS, DESC, SHARE) used in difficult conversations.
