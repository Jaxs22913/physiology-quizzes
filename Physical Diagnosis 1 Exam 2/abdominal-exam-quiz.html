Before you begin

Thirty questions built from the "Physical Examination of the Abdomen" lecture — covering the correct exam sequence and positioning, systematic inspection and auscultation (bowel sounds, bruits, friction rubs, venous hums), percussion technique (liver span, splenic percussion sign), and light/deep palpation technique for the liver, spleen, kidneys, bladder, and aorta.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Demonstrate knowledge of correct patient/examiner positioning and the appropriate order (inspection, auscultation, percussion, palpation) of the abdominal exam.
  • 2Describe and perform abdominal inspection, including contour, skin, and vascular findings.
  • 3Describe and perform abdominal auscultation, including bowel sounds, bruits, friction rubs, and venous hums.
  • 4Describe and perform abdominal percussion, including liver span and the splenic percussion sign.
  • 5Describe and perform light and deep palpation technique, including liver, spleen, kidney, bladder, and aortic assessment.
  • 6Recognize key abnormal findings (organomegaly, masses, ascites clues, vascular findings) and distinguish abdominal wall from intra-abdominal masses.
