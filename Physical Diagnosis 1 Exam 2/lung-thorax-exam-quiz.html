Before you begin

Thirty questions built from "The Physical Exam of the Lungs" lecture — covering thoracic anatomy and landmarks, the relevant history and risk factors, systematic inspection and palpation (chest expansion, tactile fremitus), percussion technique (including diaphragmatic excursion), and auscultation (normal breath sounds, adventitious sounds, and vocal resonance techniques like bronchophony and egophony).

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Review the anatomy of the thorax and lungs, including landmarks (angle of Louis, axillary lines) and lobar/fissure anatomy.
  • 2Demonstrate knowledge of the relevant pulmonary history and risk factors.
  • 3Describe and perform thoracic inspection, including chest wall shape/deformities and skin findings.
  • 4Describe and perform thoracic palpation, including chest expansion, tactile fremitus, and tracheal position.
  • 5Describe and perform thoracic percussion, including systematic technique and diaphragmatic excursion.
  • 6Describe and perform lung auscultation, including normal breath sound types, adventitious sounds, and vocal resonance (bronchophony, egophony, whispered pectoriloquy).
