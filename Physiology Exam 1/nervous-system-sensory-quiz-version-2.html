Before you begin

This exam covers Unit 9: Nervous System Intro and Sensory, spanning sensory receptor types/transduction/adaptation, neuronal pool circuits and neurotransmitter classes, the dorsal column and anterolateral somatosensory pathways, the physiology of pain (fast/slow pathways, gate theory, endogenous analgesia), thermoreception, and cerebral blood flow/CSF/blood-brain barrier physiology. Every item is drawn only from that lecture and is built around its instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Identify neurotransmitters and their properties, compare chemical and electrical synapses, and describe different functional types of sensory receptors and the steps in sensory transduction.
  • 2Describe the somatosensory pathways, the function of the thalamus, and the receptors for external sensory input, including the physiology of pain and thermoreception.
  • 3Explain the function of the blood-cerebrospinal fluid and blood-brain barriers, and the formation and function of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF).
