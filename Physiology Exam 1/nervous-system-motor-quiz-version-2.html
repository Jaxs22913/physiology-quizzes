Before you begin

This exam covers Unit 11: Nervous System Motor, spanning alpha/gamma motor neurons and motor units, muscle spindle and Golgi tendon organ physiology, spinal reflexes (stretch, withdrawal, crossed-extensor), the motor cortex and corticospinal/rubrospinal tracts, brainstem control of posture, the vestibular apparatus, and the roles of the cerebellum and basal ganglia in motor control. Every item is drawn only from that lecture and is built around its instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Review the function of the neuromuscular junction, compare and contrast spinal reflexes, and describe the function of the motor unit and different types of muscle sensors.
  • 2Describe the function of the motor pathways, the function of the basal ganglia, and the neuro components regulated by the basal ganglia.
  • 3Describe the function of the cerebellum in motor coordination, timing, and error correction.
