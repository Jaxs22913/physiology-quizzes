Before you begin

This exam covers the Respiratory System lecture, spanning the upper respiratory tract, larynx and vocal cords, trachea and bronchial tree, and the lungs (lobes, alveoli, pleura, diaphragm, and respiratory muscles). Every item is drawn only from that material and is built around the lecture's instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Compare and contrast the organs that comprise the respiratory system, and describe their anatomical structures and relations.
  • 2Compare and contrast the segments of the trachea and bronchi, and discuss the segments of the pleura and pleural cavity.
  • 3Identify the blood/nerve/lymphatic supply of the thorax and the major sections and hiatuses of the diaphragm.
