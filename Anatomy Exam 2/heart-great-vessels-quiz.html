Before you begin

This exam covers the Anatomy of the Heart and Great Vessels lecture, spanning the external/internal structure of the heart (pericardium, heart wall, chambers, valves), the cardiac conduction system and innervation, the coronary blood vessels, and the course/branches of the aorta. Every item is drawn only from that material and is built around the lecture's instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Compare and contrast the external and internal anatomical structures of the heart, and explain the cardiac conduction system.
  • 2Identify the origin and course of the main branches of the coronary arteries, and describe the cardiac nerve supply.
  • 3Describe the entire course of the aorta and name the origin of the major branches of each division.
