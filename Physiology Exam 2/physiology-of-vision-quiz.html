Before you begin

This exam covers Unit 10: Physiology of Vision, spanning the optics of the eye (refraction, accommodation, diopters), aqueous/vitreous humor and intraocular pressure, rod/cone photoreceptor physiology and phototransduction, retinal signal processing (lateral inhibition, ganglion cell types), the visual pathway to the cortex, and autonomic/motor control of the eye. Every item is drawn only from that lecture and is built around its instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Describe the special sense of vision, and compare and contrast the functions of each part of the eye.
  • 2Compare and contrast the functions of each photoreceptor of the eye, including phototransduction and color vision.
  • 3Describe the function of the optic tract pathway, including retinal processing, eye movement control, and binocular vision.
