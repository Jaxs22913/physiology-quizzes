Before you begin

This exam covers foundational anatomical terminology from the Introduction to Anatomy lecture: the anatomical position, body planes, directional/relationship terms, terms of movement, body cavities, and levels of structural organization. Every item is drawn only from that material and is built around the lecture's instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Compare and contrast the various anatomical imaginary planes of the body, the anatomical position, and the movements of the body.
  • 2Recall anatomical terms of relationship, comparison, and laterality, and describe the levels of structural organization of the body.
  • 3Compare and contrast the cavities of the body, the organs located within them, and abdominal regions and quadrants.
