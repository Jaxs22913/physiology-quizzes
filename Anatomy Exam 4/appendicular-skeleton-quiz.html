Before you begin

This exam covers the bones of the appendicular skeleton — the pectoral girdle and upper limb (clavicle, scapula, humerus, radius, ulna, carpals, metacarpals, phalanges) and the pelvic girdle and lower limb (hip bone, femur, patella, tibia, fibula, tarsals, metatarsals, phalanges) — from the Anatomy of the Appendicular Skeleton lecture. Every item is drawn only from that material and is built around the lecture's instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Identify the bones of the pectoral girdle and upper limb, and describe their key surface landmarks, articulations, and clinically relevant anatomy.
  • 2Identify the bones of the pelvic girdle and lower limb, and describe their key surface landmarks, articulations, and clinically relevant anatomy.
  • 3Compare and contrast structural differences between corresponding upper and lower limb bones, and between the male and female pelvis.
