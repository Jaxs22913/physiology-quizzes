Muscles of the Head & Neck · Vertebral Column, Rib Cage & Pelvic Floor

Before you begin

This exam covers the muscles of the head and neck (facial expression, extra-ocular, mastication, tongue, pharynx, anterior neck), the muscles of the vertebral column and rib cage/abdominal wall, the diaphragm, and the muscles of the pelvic floor, from the Axial Musculature lecture. Every item is drawn only from that material and is built around the lecture's instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Identify the muscles of facial expression, mastication, the tongue, and the extra-ocular muscles, along with their cranial nerve innervation.
  • 2Identify the extrinsic and intrinsic muscles of the back and vertebral column, and describe their layered organization and actions.
  • 3Identify the muscles of the rib cage and abdominal wall, the diaphragm, and the muscles of the pelvic floor, along with their actions and innervation.
