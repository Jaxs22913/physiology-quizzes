Before you begin

This exam covers osseous tissue histology and bone growth, the bones and landmarks of the skull (cranium, face, sutures, cranial foramina, infant skull), and the vertebral column and thoracic cage (vertebrae by region, sacrum, coccyx, ribs, sternum) from the Anatomy of the Axial Skeleton lecture. Every item is drawn only from that material and is built around the lecture's instructional objectives.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Describe the histological classification of bone and the process of bone growth, including primary and secondary ossification centers and the epiphyseal plate.
  • 2Identify the bones of the skull and their key surface landmarks, sutures, and cranial foramina, including the structures that pass through each foramen.
  • 3Identify the regions and individual bones of the vertebral column, and describe the structure and function of the thoracic cage.
