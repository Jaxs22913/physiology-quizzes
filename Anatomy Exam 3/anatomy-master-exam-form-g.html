Before you begin

This is a 50-question comprehensive master exam spanning all five Anatomy Exam 3 topics — Gastrointestinal, Endocrine, Male Reproductive, Female Reproductive, and Nephrology/Urinary. It is one of five parallel forms (A–E) of the same master exam, each drawing a different 50-question set from the same shared question pool, so no two forms are identical.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Trace the gross anatomy, peritoneal relationships, and vasculature of the GI tract, from the abdominal regions and stomach through the liver, pancreas, and hepatic portal system.
  • 2Describe the histology, gross anatomy, and calcium-regulating hormones of the endocrine organs, including the thyroid, parathyroids, adrenal glands, and pancreatic islets.
  • 3Identify the embryology, muscles, vasculature, innervation, and duct pathway of the male reproductive system, from testicular descent through the urethra.
  • 4Identify the embryology, ligaments, pelvic floor, vasculature, and histology of the female reproductive system and its peritoneal relationships.
  • 5Describe the gross and histological anatomy of the kidneys, renal vasculature, nephron physiology, and the ureters, bladder, and urethra.
