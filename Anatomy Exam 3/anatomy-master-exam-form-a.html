Before you begin

This is a 50-question comprehensive master exam spanning all five Anatomy Exam 3 topics — the gastrointestinal, endocrine, male reproductive, female reproductive, and nephrology/urinary systems. This is Form A, one of five parallel forms (A–E) drawing a different 50-question set from the same shared item pool, so your questions may not match a classmate's.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Integrate gross and histological anatomy across the GI, endocrine, male reproductive, female reproductive, and urinary systems covered in Anatomy Exam 3.
  • 2Trace major vascular, lymphatic, and ductal pathways — the hepatic portal system, renal vasculature, and semen/ovum transport — across these five systems.
  • 3Identify key gross structures, peritoneal relationships, and histological hallmarks that distinguish each organ system on practical exam material.
