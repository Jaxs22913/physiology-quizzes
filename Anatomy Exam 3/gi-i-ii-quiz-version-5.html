Before you begin

This is a fifth, independent set of questions covering Gastrointestinal Tract I & II — a fresh 45-item pass through the general structure and innervation of the GI wall, the peritoneum and mesenteric attachments (greater/lesser omentum, mesentery proper, intraperitoneal vs. retroperitoneal organs), and the abdominal quadrants/regions, for additional practice beyond the earlier quizzes.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Describe the general histologic layers of the GI tract wall and their innervation by the enteric, sympathetic, and parasympathetic nervous systems.
  • 2Distinguish visceral from parietal peritoneum, and describe the greater omentum, lesser omentum, mesentery proper, and the intraperitoneal vs. retroperitoneal organs of the abdomen.
  • 3Localize the major abdominal organs to their correct quadrant or region using the four-quadrant and nine-region schemes.
