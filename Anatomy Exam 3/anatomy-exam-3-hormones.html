Hormone Reference Guide

Every hormone named across the Endocrine, GI, Female Reproductive, Male Reproductive, and Nephrology/Urinary decks — where it's made, whether/how it's stored, and what it acts on. Kept structural: no release-trigger or feedback-loop mechanisms, since those belong to physiology, not anatomy.

Endocrine GI Female Reproductive Male Reproductive Nephrology/Urinary
On "storage": most peptide/amine hormones here are held in secretory granules inside the very cell that makes them, released on demand. Thyroid hormone is the standout exception — it's stored extracellularly in the thyroid follicle's colloid, a large reserve. Steroid hormones (cortisol, aldosterone, androgens, estrogens) generally aren't stored at all — they're synthesized from cholesterol on demand.
