Before you begin

This is a fourth, independent 280-question bank covering the gross and histological anatomy of the kidneys and urinary tract, organized into 26 topic sections spanning organization, gross anatomy, vasculature, the nephron, the juxtaglomerular apparatus, histology, and clinical/pathway application.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Describe the gross anatomy of the kidneys, including their coverings, internal structure, and relationships to surrounding structures.
  • 2Trace the arterial supply and venous drainage of the kidney, and follow the flow of filtrate through the nephron.
  • 3Identify the gross and histological anatomy of the ureters, bladder, and urethra, and the specialized cells of the juxtaglomerular apparatus.
