Before you begin

This is a genuine comprehensive Master Exam spanning all five Anatomy Exam 3 topics: GI, Endocrine, Male Reproductive, Female Reproductive, and Nephrology/Urinary. Form C is one of five parallel forms (A–E) of this exam, each drawing a different 50-question set from the same shared question pool.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Integrate gross and histological anatomy across the abdominal GI tract, peritoneum, and accessory organs (liver, pancreas, spleen, gallbladder).
  • 2Describe the endocrine glands' gross anatomy, histology, blood supply, and role in calcium regulation.
  • 3Trace the male and female reproductive tracts, their embryologic origins, vasculature, innervation, and pelvic floor relationships.
  • 4Describe the gross and histological anatomy of the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra, and follow the flow of filtrate through the nephron.
