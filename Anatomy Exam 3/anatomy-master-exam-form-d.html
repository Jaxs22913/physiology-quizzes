Before you begin

This is a 50-question comprehensive exam spanning all five Anatomy Exam 3 topics: the gastrointestinal system, the endocrine system, the male reproductive system, the female reproductive system, and the nephrology/urinary system. Every item is drawn only from the lecture material for these units. This is Form D of five parallel comprehensive forms (A–E), each drawing a different 50-question set from the same shared question pool.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Gastrointestinal system — abdominal regions, peritoneum/mesenteries, GI tract organs, hepatobiliary and pancreatic anatomy, and abdominal vasculature/lymphatics.
  • 2Endocrine system — gross and histological anatomy of the major endocrine glands, their blood supply, and calcium regulation.
  • 3Male reproductive system — embryology, pelvic floor and scrotal anatomy, blood supply, innervation, histology, and the semen pathway.
  • 4Female reproductive system — embryology, perineum, ligaments, pelvic floor, vasculature, innervation/lymphatics, and histology.
  • 5Nephrology/urinary system — kidney gross anatomy, renal vasculature, nephron structure/physiology, and the ureters, bladder & urethra.
