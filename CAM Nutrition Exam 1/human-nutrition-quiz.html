Before you begin

Twenty questions built straight from Prof. Allen's Human Nutrition 2026 lecture — covering the six classes of nutrients and key terms, the calorie value of each nutrient, calorie and metric-system calculations, obesity/BMI and diet-related leading causes of death, the Standard American Diet, and the factors that shape food habits.

You'll answer one question at a time. After each answer you'll see whether you were right, an explanation, and why the other choices don't fit. A running score and progress bar track you along the way. At the end you get a full breakdown by topic and a review of everything you missed.

  • 1Define the six classes of nutrients (carbohydrate, protein, lipid, vitamin, mineral, water) and related terms, and know the kilocalorie value each nutrient provides per gram.
  • 2Calculate total calories from a food's macronutrient breakdown and convert between metric units used in diet planning.
  • 3Identify obesity/BMI classifications, diet-and-lifestyle factors linked to leading causes of death, and the characteristics of the Standard American Diet that need improvement.
  • 4Describe the physiological, social, cultural, and economic factors that shape food habits and choices.
